In an aero engine MRO environment, a Task Control Number (TCN) is a unique identifier assigned to a specific maintenance task or work package. It enables every task performed during an engine shop visit to be planned, tracked, controlled, and recorded throughout the overhaul process.

A TCN provides full traceability by linking:

The maintenance task or operation
The engine, module, or component
The applicable maintenance manual or work instruction
The technician(s) performing the work
Inspection and quality approvals
Any associated parts, tooling, and measurements
Completion status and timestamps
Typical uses of a TCN

A TCN may be assigned to tasks such as:

Engine induction inspection
Fan module disassembly
High-pressure turbine blade inspection
Bearing replacement
Non-destructive testing (NDT)
Module assembly
Engine test cell run
Final inspection and release
Each task has its own TCN, allowing planners and production controllers to monitor progress in real time.

Why TCNs are important

They support:

Production control – monitoring which tasks are complete, in progress, or awaiting action.
Resource planning – assigning technicians, tooling, and facilities to tasks.
Regulatory compliance – providing a complete audit trail for authorities and customers.
Quality assurance – recording inspections, measurements, and sign-offs.
Turnaround time (TAT) management – identifying bottlenecks and overdue tasks on the critical path.

Best practice

To improve MRO performance, leading organisations integrate TCNs with:

Digital work instructions
Barcode or RFID tracking
OCR for automatic capture of part and serial numbers
Electronic technician and inspector sign-offs
Real-time production dashboards
Critical path and TAT analytics
This creates end-to-end visibility of every maintenance task, helping reduce delays, improve schedule adherence, and maintain complete traceability.
